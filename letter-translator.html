How It Works

Why can't I just paste the letter in?

Family court letters contain names, case numbers, children's details and information that should stay private. Describing the letter in your own words gives you the same explanation without putting sensitive information into an AI tool. It also forces you to focus on what actually matters rather than the legal language that is designed to confuse you.

Is this legal advice?

No. This tool explains what family court correspondence typically means in plain English. It does not tell you what to do, predict your outcome, or substitute for speaking to a qualified person. John Junk is not a practising solicitor or barrister. Court Compass provides information, preparation, and support.

What happens to what I type?

Your description is sent to an AI to generate the explanation and then discarded. Court Compass does not store it in a database or client records. It does not go into your name, your email list, or any CRM. The AI provider applies its own data handling policy.

The explanation said something that worries me. What do I do?

That is exactly the situation John exists for. If the explanation has raised something urgent — a deadline, an order, a safeguarding concern — do not rely on this tool alone. Book a session and get case-specific guidance from someone who has been inside family courts for thirty years.
